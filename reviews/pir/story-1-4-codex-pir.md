**2. Simplification**

Assessment: `CONCERN`

- The clearest simplification would have been: V1 M1-only, yearly chunk resume, final versioned raw artifact, manifest. Tick mode adds a second data shape and second downstream contract before V1 needs it ([prd.md:461](/c/Users/ROG/Projects/Forex%20Pipeline/_bmad-output/planning-artifacts/prd.md#L461), [downloader.py:210](/c/Users/ROG/Projects/Forex%20Pipeline/src/python/data_pipeline/downloader.py#L210), [contracts/arrow_schemas.toml:15](/c/Users/ROG/Projects/Forex%20Pipeline/contracts/arrow_schemas.toml#L15)).
- There is redundant machinery. The four incremental/merge helpers are dead in runtime, so today they add cognitive load without serving the shipped workflow ([downloader.py:454](/c/Users/ROG/Projects/Forex%20Pipeline/src/python/data_pipeline/downloader.py#L454), [downloader.py:484](/c/Users/ROG/Projects/Forex%20Pipeline/src/python/data_pipeline/downloader.py#L484), [downloader.py:507](/c/Users/ROG/Projects/Forex%20Pipeline/src/python/data_pipeline/downloader.py#L507), [downloader.py:558](/c/Users/ROG/Projects/Forex%20Pipeline/src/python/data_pipeline/downloader.py#L558)).
- Some config surface is unnecessary in its current form because it does not influence behavior. `download_timeout_seconds` and `retry_backoff_factor` increase apparent operator control without actual effect ([base.toml:68](/c/Users/ROG/Projects/Forex%20Pipeline/config/base.toml#L68), [base.toml:71](/c/Users/ROG/Projects/Forex%20Pipeline/config/base.toml#L71), [downloader.py:42](/c/Users/ROG/Projects/Forex%20Pipeline/src/python/data_pipeline/downloader.py#L42), [downloader.py:45](/c/Users/ROG/Projects/Forex%20Pipeline/src/python/data_pipeline/downloader.py#L45)).
- The shared chunk cache at `{pair}_M1_chunks` is operationally useful, but because it is pair-level rather than dataset-version-level it is harder to reason about than the final versioned artifacts. For a non-coder operator, that is extra moving-part surface with limited direct evidence value ([downloader.py:361](/c/Users/ROG/Projects/Forex%20Pipeline/src/python/data_pipeline/downloader.py#L361), [downloader.py:377](/c/Users/ROG/Projects/Forex%20Pipeline/src/python/data_pipeline/downloader.py#L377), [prd.md:189](/c/Users/ROG/Projects/Forex%20Pipeline/_bmad-output/planning-artifacts/prd.md#L189)).

**3. Forward Look**

Assessment: `CONCERN`

- The immediate handoff to Story 1.5 is solid. Story 1.4 writes `raw/{dataset_id}/vNNN/{dataset_id}.csv`, and Story 1.5’s validator loads exactly that latest-version shape ([downloader.py:654](/c/Users/ROG/Projects/Forex%20Pipeline/src/python/data_pipeline/downloader.py#L654), [validator_cli.py:57](/c/Users/ROG/Projects/Forex%20Pipeline/src/python/data_pipeline/validator_cli.py#L57), [validator_cli.py:71](/c/Users/ROG/Projects/Forex%20Pipeline/src/python/data_pipeline/validator_cli.py#L71)).
- The dataset identity is weaker than the architecture wants. Architecture says consistent sourcing is identified by `{pair}_{start_date}_{end_date}_{source}_{download_hash}`, but Story 1.4 uses `{pair}_{start}_{end}_{resolution}` and leaves the hash only inside the manifest. That is enough for provenance after opening the manifest, but not enough for artifact identity at a glance ([architecture.md:258](/c/Users/ROG/Projects/Forex%20Pipeline/_bmad-output/planning-artifacts/architecture.md#L258), [architecture.md:261](/c/Users/ROG/Projects/Forex%20Pipeline/_bmad-output/planning-artifacts/architecture.md#L261), [downloader.py:583](/c/Users/ROG/Projects/Forex%20Pipeline/src/python/data_pipeline/downloader.py#L583), [downloader.py:709](/c/Users/ROG/Projects/Forex%20Pipeline/src/python/data_pipeline/downloader.py#L709)).
- The implementation bakes in a strong assumption that only the current year ever needs refreshing; all older years are treated as stable cache. That may be acceptable for speed, but it is a brittle assumption for a pipeline whose objective is explicit attribution of divergence and reproducibility over time ([downloader.py:286](/c/Users/ROG/Projects/Forex%20Pipeline/src/python/data_pipeline/downloader.py#L286), [downloader.py:290](/c/Users/ROG/Projects/Forex%20Pipeline/src/python/data_pipeline/downloader.py#L290), [prd.md:100](/c/Users/ROG/Projects/Forex%20Pipeline/_bmad-output/planning-artifacts/prd.md#L100)).
- The optional tick path does not set later stories up cleanly. Story 1.4 emits tick-shaped data, but Story 1.6’s converter always loads `market_data` schema rather than selecting `tick_data`, so the extra capability is not actually a reliable forward contract ([downloader.py:215](/c/Users/ROG/Projects/Forex%20Pipeline/src/python/data_pipeline/downloader.py#L215), [contracts/arrow_schemas.toml:15](/c/Users/ROG/Projects/Forex%20Pipeline/contracts/arrow_schemas.toml#L15), [arrow_converter.py:406](/c/Users/ROG/Projects/Forex%20Pipeline/src/python/data_pipeline/arrow_converter.py#L406)).
- Cross-story artifact lookup is not fully stabilized. Story 1.5 saves validated data under `validated/{dataset_id}/{version}/...`, while Story 1.6’s CLI hardcodes `v001` and looks under `raw/{dataset_id}/v001/...`. That is a downstream bug, but it shows this story family did not end up with a single durable artifact contract ([quality_checker.py:770](/c/Users/ROG/Projects/Forex%20Pipeline/src/python/data_pipeline/quality_checker.py#L770), [converter_cli.py:39](/c/Users/ROG/Projects/Forex%20Pipeline/src/python/data_pipeline/converter_cli.py#L39), [converter_cli.py:43](/c/Users/ROG/Projects/Forex%20Pipeline/src/python/data_pipeline/converter_cli.py#L43)).

**Overall**

`REVISIT`

The story helps on artifact persistence and baseline observability, but it misses some of the system’s core intent in practice: requested date ranges are not honored precisely, incremental validation/merge logic is mostly not in the runtime path, timeout control is not real, and tick support expanded scope before the downstream contract was stable.
