**2. Simplification**

Assessment: `ADEQUATE`

- The biggest simplification opportunity is the baseline loader. `load_epic3_baseline()` creates synthetic market data, strategy spec, and cost model if prior artifacts are absent ([test_epic5_pipeline_proof.py:120-144](/c/Users/ROG/Projects/Forex Pipeline/tests/e2e/test_epic5_pipeline_proof.py#L120), [test_epic5_pipeline_proof.py:147-261](/c/Users/ROG/Projects/Forex Pipeline/tests/e2e/test_epic5_pipeline_proof.py#L147)). That makes the proof more self-contained, but it also means this story can succeed without proving real compatibility with Epic 3 artifacts. A simpler and more objective-serving option would be one checked-in reference fixture or a hard fail when the upstream baseline contract is missing.
- The operator-review area is somewhat over-built for the value it gives. Several tests create a pipeline state file, call one helper, and assert a few keys ([test_epic5_pipeline_proof.py:1007-1156](/c/Users/ROG/Projects/Forex Pipeline/tests/e2e/test_epic5_pipeline_proof.py#L1007)). A single parameterized contract test could probably cover accept/reject/refine/status with less duplication.
- The checkpoint suite is the clearest case of partial complexity. The class docstring explicitly says it proves only the checkpoint contract, not real interrupt/resume ([test_epic5_pipeline_proof.py:1339-1354](/c/Users/ROG/Projects/Forex Pipeline/tests/e2e/test_epic5_pipeline_proof.py#L1339)). If the live interrupt/resume path cannot be exercised yet, a smaller and more honest shape would be one contract test plus a documented deferred acceptance item, instead of three AC #10-branded tests that do not demonstrate recomputation avoidance.
- I do not see major abstraction bloat in the core proof path. The fixture chain optimization -> validation -> scoring is straightforward and aligns with the architecture’s stage model ([architecture.md:417-421](/c/Users/ROG/Projects/Forex Pipeline/_bmad-output/planning-artifacts/architecture.md#L417), [test_epic5_pipeline_proof.py:467-558](/c/Users/ROG/Projects/Forex Pipeline/tests/e2e/test_epic5_pipeline_proof.py#L467)).

**3. Forward Look**

Assessment: `CONCERN`

- The downstream direction is correct: Story 5.7 anchors Epic 6 handoff on `scoring_manifest.json`, and the saved fixture is a thin wrapper around that contract ([story 5.7:73-75](/c/Users/ROG/Projects/Forex Pipeline/_bmad-output/implementation-artifacts/5-7-e2e-pipeline-proof-optimization-validation.md#L73), [story 5.7:172-173](/c/Users/ROG/Projects/Forex Pipeline/_bmad-output/implementation-artifacts/5-7-e2e-pipeline-proof-optimization-validation.md#L172), [test_epic5_pipeline_proof.py:1590-1617](/c/Users/ROG/Projects/Forex Pipeline/tests/e2e/test_epic5_pipeline_proof.py#L1590)).
- The concern is that the proof does not enforce the full downstream payload the story itself says Epic 6 needs. AC #13 calls for candidate IDs, decisions, ratings, gate outcomes, artifact refs, and provenance hashes ([story 5.7:75](/c/Users/ROG/Projects/Forex Pipeline/_bmad-output/implementation-artifacts/5-7-e2e-pipeline-proof-optimization-validation.md#L75)). But `test_epic6_fixture_saved()` only asserts a handful of top-level keys exist ([test_epic5_pipeline_proof.py:1624-1629](/c/Users/ROG/Projects/Forex Pipeline/tests/e2e/test_epic5_pipeline_proof.py#L1624)). That leaves downstream stories dependent on upstream executors “just happening” to include the richer fields.
- The same issue appears in provenance. `test_manifest_chain_integrity()` only requires `dataset_hash`, `config_hash`, and `strategy_spec_hash` across manifests ([test_epic5_pipeline_proof.py:1538-1547](/c/Users/ROG/Projects/Forex Pipeline/tests/e2e/test_epic5_pipeline_proof.py#L1538)). For pipeline growth, I would expect `cost_model_version`, `strategy_spec_version`, and config-specific hashes to be part of the enforced contract, not optional extras.
- There are assumptions that may not hold as the pipeline grows:
- The proof tolerates missing Rust by skipping major portions of the suite ([test_epic5_pipeline_proof.py:472](/c/Users/ROG/Projects/Forex Pipeline/tests/e2e/test_epic5_pipeline_proof.py#L472), [test_epic5_pipeline_proof.py:1724](/c/Users/ROG/Projects/Forex Pipeline/tests/e2e/test_epic5_pipeline_proof.py#L1724), [story 5.7:520](/c/Users/ROG/Projects/Forex Pipeline/_bmad-output/implementation-artifacts/5-7-e2e-pipeline-proof-optimization-validation.md#L520)). That is workable today, but weakens the story as a dependable contract gate for later epics.
- The operator contract is loose. `load_evidence_pack()` may return `None`, and status assertions do not check “what passed, what failed, and why,” which FR40 explicitly requires ([prd.md:517-518](/c/Users/ROG/Projects/Forex Pipeline/_bmad-output/planning-artifacts/prd.md#L517), [test_epic5_pipeline_proof.py:962-970](/c/Users/ROG/Projects/Forex Pipeline/tests/e2e/test_epic5_pipeline_proof.py#L962), [test_epic5_pipeline_proof.py:1007-1038](/c/Users/ROG/Projects/Forex Pipeline/tests/e2e/test_epic5_pipeline_proof.py#L1007)).
- AC #10 remains only partially protective for downstream resume-dependent stories. The synthesis report explicitly records live interrupt/resume as deferred ([synthesis-report.md:100-104](/c/Users/ROG/Projects/Forex Pipeline/reviews/synthesis/5-7-e2e-pipeline-proof-optimization-validation-synthesis-report.md#L100)).

**Overall**

Assessment: `OBSERVE`

This story is directionally aligned. It materially improves reproducibility and artifact discipline, and it puts the right downstream contract at the center. The main observations are that operator-confidence proof is shallower than the PRD/architecture expect, and the Epic 6 handoff contract is not asserted as tightly as the story text claims.
